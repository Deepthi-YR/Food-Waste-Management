# Local-Food-Waste-Management-System
Labmentix-Internship

# Business Objective
To reduce food wastage by connecting food providers (restaurants, grocery stores, supermarkets, etc.) with receivers (NGOs, community centers, needy individuals) through a centralized platform. 

# Business Problem
Current Problems
1.	Excess food is wasted. 
2.	NGOs don't know where food is available. 
3.	No centralized platform exists. 
4.	Food distribution is inefficient. 
5.	Providers and receivers cannot easily connect. 
6.	No proper analysis of food donation trends. 

# Skills
  •	Python
  •	SQL
  •	Streamlit

# Approach
1. Data Preparation
2. Database Creation
  •	Store food availability data in SQL tables.
  •	Implement CRUD operations for updating, adding, and removing records.
3. Data Analysis.
  •	Identify food wastage trends based on categories, locations, and expiry dates.
  •	Generate reports for effective food distribution.
4. Application Development
Develop a Streamlit-based user interface to:
  •	Display output of the 15 SQL queries.
  •	Provided filtering options based on city, provider, food type, and meal type.
  •	Show contact details of providers for direct coordination.
5. Deployment
  •	Deploy the Streamlit application for accessibility and real-time interaction

# Business Insights
1. Food Availability:Which city has the most food available?
Insight:Bangalore has the highest total food quantity available.This indicates a strong network of food providers and donation activity in the city.

Business Impact:Bangalore can act as a model city for food donation programs.Surplus food can be redistributed to nearby regions with lower availability.

2. Food Waste Analysis:Which meal gets wasted the most?
Insight:Dinner meals show the highest amount of unused food.This may be due to overproduction by hotels, restaurants, and catering services.

Business Impact:Providers can improve demand forecasting and meal planning.Targeted collection drives can reduce food wastage.

3. Provider Analysis:Which provider contributes the most food?
Insight:Hotels contribute the largest share of donated food.Large-scale food preparation results in more surplus food availability.

Business Impact:Hotels are key stakeholders in the food redistribution ecosystem.Stronger partnerships with hotels can increase donation volume.

4. Receiver Analysis:Which receiver claims the most food?
Insight:NGOs and food banks account for the highest number of food claims.These organizations efficiently distribute food to beneficiaries.

Business Impact:Supporting active receivers can improve food utilization rates.Priority allocation can be provided to high-performing organizations.

5. Claims Analysis:What percentage of claims are completed?
Insight:A high completion percentage indicates efficient coordination between providers and receivers.A low percentage may indicate transportation or communication issues.

Business Impact:Monitoring claim completion helps evaluate operational efficiency.

6. Demand Analysis:Which city has the highest food demand?

Insight:Cities with the highest number of food claims represent areas of greatest demand.These cities require more frequent food collection and distribution efforts.

Business Impact:Resources and volunteers can be strategically allocated to high-demand locations.

# Business Recommendations
Recommendation 1:Increase NGO Partnerships in High-Wastage Cities
Cities generating large amounts of surplus food should collaborate with more NGOs and food banks to ensure timely food redistribution and minimize waste.

Recommendation 2:Recognize Top Food Contributors
Providers contributing the highest quantities of food should receive recognition through certificates, awards, or public acknowledgments to encourage continued participation.

Recommendation 3:Implement Automated Expiry Alerts
Automated notifications should be sent to providers and receivers before food reaches its expiry time to improve food utilization and reduce wastage.

Recommendation 4:Strengthen Collection in High-Demand Areas
Additional collection vehicles, volunteers, and distribution centers should be deployed in cities with high food demand to ensure efficient food delivery.

Recommendation 5:Predict Surplus Food Using Analytics
Historical donation data can be analyzed to predict surplus food generation and optimize collection schedules.

Recommendation 6:Improve Claim Processing Efficiency
Track pending and rejected claims regularly to identify bottlenecks and improve overall claim completion rates.

Recommendation 7:Promote Real-Time Food Availability Tracking
A live dashboard showing available food, claim status, and expiry information can help receivers respond more quickly to donation opportunities.
